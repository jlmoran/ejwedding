What's the go

Erin & Jacob just want to have a casual wedding.

The ceremony and reception will happen all at once.

Imagine, if you will, a more formal 2-for-1 schnitzel night at your local RSL. There will be finger food, drinks and music, but at the end of the party Erin & Jacob will be married.

Getting there

For those of you who aren't from Newcastle, there are a few ways of getting there.

  1. Drive yourself

    There is free street parking available after 12pm on Saturdays around the venue, although it can be busy.

  2. Let the government drive

    Public transport on the weekends is next to non-existant however, the venue is on Hunter St meaning buses run along there occasionally.

  3. Let a uni student drive you

    Ubers are sometimes hard to find, but they do exist so make sure you have the app. Use Jacob's code when downloading the app and save some money.

  4. Let a taxi drive you

    Do you like overpaying for a guy to tell you how bad the Knights are while taking you via Sydney? Then a taxi is probably your best option.

  5. Take a catapult

    Don't do this...or do, it's your life.

What to wear

Smart casual.

However, it's really up to you. Erin & Jacob will be wearing clothes, so it's probably advisable to follow their lead with that.

Gifts

We do not expect a gift, but there will be a wishing well for the honeymoon.

We know it's not tradition, but feel free to just give money, as money can be exchanged for goods and services.
